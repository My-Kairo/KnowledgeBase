WEBCHAT TEAM

Data protection requirements

If data protection is not completed or passed, we should not be disclosing any account information with the customer. Ensure approved security questions have been asked from the customer in order to discuss and divulge sensitive account information.

What can be deemed as sensitive:

  • Contact information
  • Contract end dates
  • Financila information
  • Meter details
  • Account activity dates
  • Open tickets or any updates
  • Account status (terminated / closed / active
  • Complaints status / activity

Confirm customer's name.

Always confirm who you are speaking to by obtaining the customers full name and surname and across comparing to the name we hold on the account to ensure this matches the info on the account and it is correct before proceeding with security questions

Pasword

Refrain from attempting to give hints or assist the customer with remembering the password.

If the customer does not remember or does not know the password, then you will be required to move onto using using the manual process of asking approved security questions

Approved security questions

  • Email address
  • Date of birth
  • Last invoice amount
  • Fist line of home address
  • Home address post code

PLEASE TAKE NOTE FOR THE EMAIL AND HOME ADDRESS ON THE FOLLOWING;
Email address
  • The customer must confirnm the primary email we hold on file (contact card)
  • If there are two email address on the file, the customer must confirm the address linked to their name
  • If it is a limited company, the email address confirmed can be accepted provided the company domain matches, the the business is registered on the companie's house and they have confirmed their position / role within the business.
Home address
  • When confirming home address we need to make sure it is the home address and not the business address
  • If the address under the contact card defers from the address under the agreements tab, then this means the address on the contact card is regarded as home address and can be used to pas GDPR with the customer.
  • If the address on both the agreements tab and on the contact card are aligned and match up, then this means that we only hold the business/site address for the customer and unfortunately we cannot pass the customer using this address as a question.

If there is an active DD on the account, we would expect the last 3 digits of the bank account number to match the active details. Always use the latest banking details we have on the account to cross reference with what the customer has provided.

If the customer is not named on the account and the business is the limited company, then we are required to ask the postion the customer is serving on the business, their business email address (their email domain should match the domain existing on the account), last invoice amount or the last 3 digits of the bank account number if the DD is active. However, if the customer is not named on the account and the business is not a limited compony then advise the customer to send us an LOA to update their account in order to pass DPA.
